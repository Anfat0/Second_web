Deep Brain Stimulator (DBS) Development. (AI)

At Aarhus University, I contributed to the design and implementation of a Deep Brain Stimulator (DBS) using OrCAD software. My role involved state-of-the-art research on DBS implants and electronic components, programming and debugging the ATXMEGA32E5 microcontroller using C language, and conducting LTspice simulations to select optimal off-the-shelf components for the stimulation circuit. Additionally, I performed soldering of surface-mount devices (SMDs) on a multi-layer PCB for a wearable device.

DBS Circuit Simulation and Design

In the picture, you can see the diagram of the off-the-shelf components required to build the Deep Brain Stimulator (DBS). A simulation was carried out in LTspice to ensure that the circuit performed its intended functions effectively. The diagram includes an H-bridge of switches, which enabled both monopolar and bipolar stimulation. It also allowed for voltage and current stimulation using various waveforms.

PCB Assembly

After completing the schematic design and PCB layout in OrCAD software, I moved on to soldering the surface-mount (SMD) components. The images show various iterations of the PCB, each improved upon with every version. The process of soldering numerous components helped me hone my soldering skills, and I also learned how to create custom footprints from scratch.

Microcontroller Programming and Waveform Generation

Programming and debugging of the microcontroller were done using C language. The graphs display some of the different waveforms generated by the programmable current generator developed for this application. The waveforms are biphasic to ensure charge balancing, including rectangular, triangular, exponential rise, and exponential decay shapes. While DBS typically uses rectangular waveforms for stimulation, this project aimed to experiment with waveforms that more closely resemble the brain's natural electrochemical signals. After achieving positive results with the PCB prototype, a smaller PCB was designed for animal experiments.
